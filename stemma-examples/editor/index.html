Word editor

Open a .docx and edit it with its real formatting — fonts, sizes, colors, highlights, alignment, inline images — rendered from stemma's /rich projection. In Suggesting mode edits land as tracked changes; in Editing mode, directly. Commits are optimistic, so a far server's round-trip stays invisible.

Open a .docx — or click a sample — to begin.

How this example works

  1. Open a .docx — it renders with its full character/paragraph formatting from stemma's rich projection.
  2. Pick a mode. Suggesting writes edits as tracked changes; Editing bakes them in directly.
  3. Format a selection with B / I / U / S (or ⌘/Ctrl+B/I/U) — the marks ride along when you commit, round-tripping through stemma's typed replace.
  4. Edit text, then Commit — each changed block becomes one guard-pinned op. The redline shows instantly and syncs in the background.
  5. Export the document exactly as you see it.

Tables render as real tables, images inline, equations via MathJax, headers/footers and page borders from the section. The toolbar authors bold/italic/underline/strike plus font, size, color, highlight (via the engine's set_format op — color/highlight as a tracked rPrChange) and alignment/indent (via set_para_format).
